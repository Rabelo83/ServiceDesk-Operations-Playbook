# Service Desk Analyst III - Strategic Objectives Matrix

This document maps the high-level progression goals for this 90-day plan directly to the core HR requirements listed for the internal **Level III IT** position. 

By executing these objectives, you bridge the gap between Customer Service operations and Enterprise IT infrastructure.

---

## 🎯 Objective 1: Prove Enterprise Device Management (MDM)
**The Analyst III Requirement:** Maintain, configure, and troubleshoot endpoint management systems (Microsoft Intune, SCCM, Apple Business Manager).
*   **Your Disadvantage:** As a CSS, you do not have production access to the City's Intune tenant.
*   **Your Strategic Goal:** Build and configure an isolated, enterprise-grade Intune environment using the free Microsoft 365 E5 developer sandbox. Prove you can enroll devices, push compliance policies, and package Win32 apps safely to completely negate the lack of production access.

## 🎯 Objective 2: Establish Technical Leadership & Mentoring
**The Analyst III Requirement:** Provide mentorship, cross-training, and escalation support for Level I and Level II technicians.
*   **Your Disadvantage:** You aren't on the Service Desk team, so you can't mentor junior IT staff.
*   **Your Strategic Goal:** Prove you are a technical leader in *any* environment by explicitly cross-training your current Customer Service teammates on the advanced systems you built (Odoo, SMS workflows). Treat your CSS peers like Level I techs and document your training. 

## 🎯 Objective 3: Demonstrate Systems Innovation & Automation
**The Analyst III Requirement:** Analyze trends, resolve complex overarching problems, and build scripts/automation to improve Service Desk efficiency.
*   **Your Strategic Advantage:** You are already doing this!
*   **Your Strategic Goal:** Frame your work on the **RTS Text SMS Prediction System** and the **Odoo Free Fare** project not merely as "customer service tasks," but as high-level IT workflow automation. Document these projects in your playbook as undeniable proof of your systems architecture capability.

## 🎯 Objective 4: Adopt ITIL & Enterprise Networking Standards
**The Analyst III Requirement:** Strong understanding of enterprise networking (CompTIA Network+ expected) and IT Service Management (ITIL) principles.
*   **Your Disadvantage:** You may lack formal enterprise network troubleshooting (VPNs, Subnets, 802.1x) experience on paper.
*   **Your Strategic Goal:** Actively study the Network+ exam objectives to confidently discuss enterprise infrastructure during the interview. When writing your process documentation, use formal ITIL terminology (Incident Management, Root Cause Analysis, Change Management) so you "speak the language" of the hiring IT Manager perfectly.

## 🎯 Objective 5: Build an Undeniable Public Portfolio 
**The Analyst III Requirement:** Proven ability to create, govern, and maintain pristine IT Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) and knowledge base articles.
*   **Your Strategic Goal:** Because you are transferring from the "outside," your resume isn't enough. You will compile your lab configurations, your CSS automation achievements, and your newly drafted SOPs into your GitHub Playbook. This public, interactive dashboard serves as physical proof that you already think and operate at a Level III capacity.
